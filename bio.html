Conductor RYAN TANI recently completed his two-year tenure as the Orchestral Conducting Fellow for the Yale Philharmonia under Music Director Peter Oundjian, where he was the 2021 recipient of the Dean's Prize for artistic excellence in his graduating class. Ryan recently concluded his tenure as Music Director of the Occasional Symphony in Baltimore. A fierce advocate of new music, Ryan curated over 20 commissions from Baltimore-based composers during his four-year directorship of OS. As Resident Conductor of the New Music New Haven series, he collaborated, under guidance of Aaron Jay Kernis, with Yale University composition students and faculty. Ryan currently serves as Cover Conductor for the Colorado and Bozeman Symphonies.

Highlights of the 2021-2022 season include cover engagements with the Colorado Symphony Orchestra, as well as conducting appearances with the Bozeman Symphony, Mississippi Valley Orchestra, Choir of the Cathedral of the Madeleine, and the Yale Philharmonia.

During his tenure as Co-Founder and Music Director of the Concerto Chamber Orchestra from 2012-2015, Ryan helped positively shape the orchestral scene in the Los Angeles area by programming exciting concerts to showcase emerging composers and musicians both within and outside the USC Thornton School of Music. The infrastructure that he and his Board put together thrives to this day.

In addition to being a conductor, Ryan maintains an active career as a tenor. In Los Angeles, he was in demand as a performer and in several professional early-music and new-music groups, including the USC Chamber Singers, the USC Thornton Baroque Sinfonia, Horizon Music Group, Tesserae LA, De Angelis, Flos Campi, the Choir of Saint James in the City, and Thornton Edge.

Ryan is a graduate of the Peabody Institute where he studied conducting with Marin Alsop and Markand Thakar, and of the University of Southern California, where he studied voice with Gary Glaze. In 2015, he was declared the winner of the ACDA Undergraduate Student Conducting Competition at their national conference in Salt Lake City, Utah.
